of lines 4 and 5 between these algorithms are proved as

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {\left\{ \begin{array}{ll}x^{\prime a_1|s_i}\leftarrow x^{\prime a_1|s_i} + \gamma \\ \mathbf{x}'\leftarrow \mathrm{Norm}(\mathbf{x}')\\ \Delta^{a_1|s_i}\leftarrow \frac{u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x}',\mathbf{y}) - u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})}{\gamma}\\ \Leftrightarrow \left\{ \begin{array}{ll}x^{\prime a_1|s_i}\leftarrow x^{\prime a_1|s_i} + (1 - x^{\prime a_1|s_i})\gamma +O(\gamma^2)\\ x^{\prime a_2|s_i}\leftarrow x^{\prime a_2|s_i} - x^{\prime a_2|s_i}\gamma +O(\gamma^2)\\ \Delta^{a_1|s_i}\leftarrow \frac{u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x}',\mathbf{y}) - u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})}{\gamma}\\ \Leftrightarrow \left\{ \begin{array}{ll}x_i'\leftarrow x_i' + (1 - x_i')\gamma \\ \Delta_i\leftarrow \frac{u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x}',\mathbf{y}) - u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})}{\gamma}\\ \Leftrightarrow \left\{ \begin{array}{ll}x_i'\leftarrow x_i' + \gamma \\ \Delta_i\leftarrow (1 - x_i')\frac{u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x}',\mathbf{y}) - u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})}{\gamma} \end{array} \right. \end{array} \right. \end{array} \right.} \end{array} \right.} \end{array} \tag{A30}
$$

Here, we ignore terms of  $O(\gamma^2)$  and use the definition of  $\mathbf{x}$  (i.e.,  $x_{i}^{\prime} = x^{\prime a_{1}|s_{i}} = 1 - x^{\prime a_{2}|s_{i}}$ ) between Eqs. (A31) and (A32). Thus, we use the continualized version of this algorithm;

$$
\dot{\pmb{x}} = \pmb {x}\circ (\mathbf{1} - \pmb {x})\circ \frac{\partial}{\partial\pmb{x}} u^{\mathrm{st}}(\pmb {x},\pmb {y}). \tag{A34}
$$

# B.2 Approximation of learning dynamics

In Section 4.2 and 5.1, we introduce a method to approximate the learning dynamics up to  $k$  - th order terms for deviations from the Nash equilibrium. The stationary state condition of the one- memory two- action game is given by

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = \pmb {M}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}},}\\ & {\pmb {M} = \left( \begin{array}{llll}x_{1}y_{1} & x_{2}y_{2} & x_{3}y_{3} & x_{4}y_{4}\\ x_{1}\tilde{y}_{1} & x_{2}\tilde{y}_{2} & x_{3}\tilde{y}_{3} & x_{4}\tilde{y}_{4}\\ \tilde{x}_{1}y_{1} & \tilde{x}_{2}\tilde{y}_{2} & \tilde{x}_{3}\tilde{y}_{3} & \tilde{x}_{4}\tilde{y}_{4}\\ \tilde{x}_{1}\tilde{y}_{1} & \tilde{x}_{2}\tilde{y}_{2} & \tilde{x}_{3}\tilde{y}_{3} & \tilde{x}_{4}\tilde{y}_{4} \end{array} \right).} \end{array} \tag{A35}
$$

Here, for any variable  $\mathcal{X}$  , we define  $\tilde{\mathcal{A}} \coloneqq 1 - \mathcal{A}$  . In addition, let us denote  $O(\delta^k)$  term in any variable  $\mathcal{X}$  as  $\mathcal{X}^{(k)}$  . The neighbor of the Nash equilibrium, by substituting  $\pmb {x} = x^{*}\mathbf{1} + \delta$  and  $\pmb {y} = y^{*}\mathbf{1} + \epsilon$  , we can decompose  $\begin{array}{r}M = \sum_{k = 0}^{2}M^{(k)} \end{array}$  as

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & M = \underbrace{(\pmb{x}^{*}\circ\pmb{y}^{*})}_{= M^{(0)}}\otimes \mathbf{1} + \underbrace{(\pmb{y}^{*}\circ\mathbf{1}_{x})\otimes\pmb{\delta} + (\pmb{x}^{*}\circ\mathbf{1}_{y})\otimes\pmb{\epsilon} + \mathbf{1}_{z}\otimes(\pmb{\delta}\circ\pmb{\epsilon})}_{= M^{(1)}}\\ & \pmb{x}^{*}\coloneqq (\pmb{x}^{*},\pmb{x}^{*},\tilde{\pmb{x}}^{*},\tilde{\pmb{x}}^{*}),\qquad \pmb{y}^{*}\coloneqq (\pmb{y}^{*},\tilde{\pmb{y}}^{*},\pmb{y}^{*},\tilde{\pmb{y}}^{*}),\\ & \mathbf{1}_{x}\coloneqq (+1, + 1, - 1, - 1)^{\mathrm{T}},\qquad \mathbf{1}_{y}\coloneqq (+1, - 1, + 1, - 1)^{\mathrm{T}},\qquad \mathbf{1}_{z}\coloneqq \mathbf{1}_{x}\circ \mathbf{1}_{y} = (+1, - 1, - 1, + 1)^{\mathrm{T}}. \end{array} \tag{A38}
$$

In the same way, we can decompose  $\begin{array}{r}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\simeq \sum_{k = 0}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(k)} \end{array}$  as

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(0)} = \pmb{M}^{(0)}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(0)} = \underbrace{(\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(0)}\cdot\mathbf{1})}_{= 1}\pmb{x}^{*}\circ \pmb{y}^{*}\eqqcolon \pmb{p}^{*},}\\ & {\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(1)} = \pmb{M}^{(1)}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(0)} = (\pmb {\delta}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})\pmb{y}^{*}\circ \mathbf{1}_{x} + (\pmb {\epsilon}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})\pmb{x}^{*}\circ \mathbf{1}_{y},}\\ & {\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(2)} = \pmb{M}^{(2)}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(0)} + \pmb{M}^{(1)}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}(1)}}\\ & {\qquad = (\pmb {\delta}\circ \pmb {\epsilon}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})\mathbf{1}_{z} + \{(\pmb {\delta}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})(\pmb {\delta}\circ \pmb{y}^{*}\cdot \mathbf{1}_{x}) + (\pmb {\epsilon}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})(\pmb {\delta}\circ \pmb{x}^{*}\cdot \mathbf{1}_{y})\} \pmb{y}^{*}\circ \mathbf{1}_{x}}\\ & {\qquad +\{(\pmb {\delta}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})(\pmb {\epsilon}\circ \pmb{y}^{*}\cdot \mathbf{1}_{x}) + (\pmb {\epsilon}\cdot \pmb{p}^{*})(\pmb {\epsilon}\circ \pmb{x}^{*}\cdot \mathbf{1}_{y})\} \pmb{x}^{*}\circ \mathbf{1}_{y}.} \end{array} \tag{A43}
$$
