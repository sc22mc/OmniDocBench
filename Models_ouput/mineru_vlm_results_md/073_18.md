We can decompose  $\begin{array}{r}\dot{\pmb{\delta}}\simeq \sum_{k = 0}\dot{\pmb{\delta}}^{(k)} \end{array}$  and  $\begin{array}{r}\dot{\epsilon}\simeq \sum_{k = 0}\dot{\epsilon}^{(k)} \end{array}$  as

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {\dot{\delta}^{(0)} = +x^{*}\tilde{x}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(1)}}{\partial\delta},}\\ & {\dot{\delta}^{(1)} = +x^{*}\tilde{x}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(2)}}{\partial\delta} -(x^{*} - \tilde{x}^{*})\delta \circ \frac{\partial u^{(1)}}{\partial\delta},}\\ & {\dot{\delta}^{(2)} = +x^{*}\tilde{x}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(3)}}{\partial\delta} -(x^{*} - \tilde{x}^{*})\delta \circ \frac{\partial u^{(2)}}{\partial\delta} +\delta \circ \delta \circ \frac{\partial u^{(1)}}{\partial\delta},}\\ & {\dot{\delta}^{(3)} = +x^{*}\tilde{x}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(4)}}{\partial\delta} -(x^{*} - \tilde{x}^{*})\delta \circ \frac{\partial u^{(3)}}{\partial\delta} +\delta \circ \delta \circ \frac{\partial u^{(2)}}{\partial\delta},}\\ & {\dot{\epsilon}^{(0)} = -y^{*}\tilde{y}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(1)}}{\partial\epsilon},}\\ & {\dot{\epsilon}^{(1)} = -y^{*}\tilde{y}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(2)}}{\partial\epsilon} +(y^{*} - \tilde{y}^{*})\epsilon \circ \frac{\partial u^{(1)}}{\partial\epsilon},}\\ & {\dot{\epsilon}^{(2)} = -y^{*}\tilde{y}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(3)}}{\partial\epsilon} +(y^{*} - \tilde{y}^{*})\epsilon \circ \frac{\partial u^{(2)}}{\partial\epsilon} -\epsilon \circ \epsilon \circ \frac{\partial u^{(1)}}{\partial\epsilon},}\\ & {\dot{\epsilon}^{(3)} = -y^{*}\tilde{y}^{*}\frac{\partial u^{(4)}}{\partial\epsilon} +(y^{*} - \tilde{y}^{*})\epsilon \circ \frac{\partial u^{(3)}}{\partial\epsilon} -\epsilon \circ \epsilon \circ \frac{\partial u^{(2)}}{\partial\epsilon}.} \end{array} \tag{A62}
$$

In cases of one- memory penny- matching games, the solution is obtained if we substitute

$$
\pmb{x}^{*} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{1},\qquad \pmb{y}^{*} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{1},\qquad \pmb{p}^{*} = \frac{1}{4}\mathbf{1}. \tag{A67}
$$

# B.3 Method to Calculate the Stationary State

Regarding Section 5.1, we use an analytical solution of the stationary state, which is known only in the case of two- action one- memory games as

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {p_1^{\mathrm{st}} = k\{(x_4 + (x_3 - x_4)y_3)(y_4 + (y_2 - y_4)x_2) - x_3y_2(x_2 - x_4)(y_3 - y_4)\} ,}\\ & {p_2^{\mathrm{st}} = k\{(x_4 + (x_3 - x_4)y_4)(\tilde{y}_3 - (y_1 - y_3)x_1) - x_4\tilde{y}_1(x_1 - x_3)(y_3 - y_4)\} ,}\\ & {p_3^{\mathrm{st}} = k\{(x_2 - (x_1 - x_2)y_1)(y_4 + (y_2 - y_4)x_4) - \tilde{x}_1y_4(x_2 - x_4)(y_1 - y_2)\} ,}\\ & {p_4^{\mathrm{st}} = k\{(x_2 - (x_1 - x_2)y_2)(\tilde{y}_3 - (y_1 - y_3)x_3) - \tilde{x}_2\tilde{y}_3(x_1 - x_3)(y_1 - y_2)\} ,}\\ & {k = \frac{1}{p_1^{\mathrm{st}} + p_2^{\mathrm{st}} + p_3^{\mathrm{st}} + p_4^{\mathrm{st}}},} \end{array} \tag{A68}
$$

under the notation in Assumption 1.

In other parts (Sections 5.2 and 5.3), we calculate the stationary state of a Markov transition matrix  $M$  by the power iteration method. Compared to the analytical solution  $\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}$ , the computational solution  $\hat{\pmb{p}}^{\mathrm{st}}$  is accurate except for  $10^{- 9}$  error in  $L^2$  norm (i.e.,  $\| \hat{\pmb{p}}^{\mathrm{st}} - \pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\| _2 \leq 10^{- 9}$ ).
