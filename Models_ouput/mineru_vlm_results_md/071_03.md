The dual isogeny  $\hat{\phi}$  is such that  $\phi \circ \hat{\phi} = \times 3$ , where  $\times 3$  is the multiplication- by- 3 map. The map defined by the dual isogeny  $\hat{\phi}$  is given in the proof of Lemma 4.5.

Consider now the exact sequence ([15, Section X.4, Remark 4.7])

$$
0\to \hat{E}_D(\mathbb{Q})[\hat{\phi} /\phi (E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q})[3])\to \hat{E}_D(\mathbb{Q}) / \phi (E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}))\stackrel {\hat{\phi}}{\to} \tag{5}
$$

$$
\begin{array}{r}\frac{\hat{\phi}}{\to} E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}) / 3E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q})\to E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}) / \hat{\phi} (\hat{E}_D(\mathbb{Q}))\to 0. \end{array}
$$

Since both  $\ker_{\hat{\phi}}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}) = T_{D'}$  and  $\ker_{\hat{\phi}}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}) = \overline{T}_D$  contain no non- trivial rational point of order 3, the first quotient group of  $[\underline{5} ]$  vanishes and the rank  $r(E_{D'})$  of  $E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q})$  equals

$$
r(E_{D'}) = \dim_{\mathbb{F}_3}(E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}) / 3E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q})). \tag{6}
$$

Consider the short exact sequence

$$
0\to \mathcal{T}_{D'}\to E_{D'}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}})\stackrel {\phi}{\to}\hat{E}_D(\overline{\mathbb{Q}})\to 0. \tag{7}
$$

From this, we obtain the long exact cohomology sequence which gives in particular the following

$$
0\to \hat{E}_D(\mathbb{Q}) / \phi (E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}))\stackrel {\phi}{\to}H^1 (G_{\mathbb{Q}},\mathcal{T}_{D'})\to H^1 (G_{\mathbb{Q}},E_{D'}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}))[q]\to 0. \tag{8}
$$

By localising at each prime  $p$ , we obtain the following commutative diagram, where  $res_{p}$  is the usual restriction map:

$$
\begin{array}{r l r} & {} & {0\to \hat{E}_{D}(\mathbb{Q}) / \phi (E_{D^{\prime}}(\mathbb{Q}))\to H^{1}(G_{\mathbb{Q}},\mathcal{T}_{D^{\prime}})\to H^{1}(G_{\mathbb{Q}},E_{D^{\prime}}(\overline{{\mathbb{Q}}}))[\phi ]\to 0}\\ & {} & {\downarrow \qquad \downarrow^{r e s_{p}}\qquad \downarrow^{r e s_{p}}}\\ & {} & {0\to \hat{E}_{D}(\mathbb{Q}_{p}) / \phi (E_{D^{\prime}}(\mathbb{Q}_{p}))\to H^{1}(G_{\mathbb{Q}_{p}},\mathcal{T}_{D^{\prime}})\to H^{1}(G_{\mathbb{Q}_{p}},E_{D^{\prime}}(\overline{{\mathbb{Q}}}_{p}))[\phi ]\to 0} \end{array}
$$

Definition 2.1. The Selmer group of  $E_{D'}$  relative to the isogeny  $\phi$  is

$\mathcal{S}_{\phi}(E_{D'}) = \{x\in H^{1}(G_{\mathbb{Q}},\mathcal{T}_{D'})\mid res_{p}(x)\in \mathrm{Im}(\hat{E}_{D}(\mathbb{Q}_{p}) / \phi (E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}_{p})))$  for all  $p\}$

The Tate- Shafarevich group of  $E_{D'}$  can now be defined as

$$
\mathrm{III}(E_{D'}) = \{x\in H^1 (G_{\mathbb{Q}},E_{D'}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}))\mid res_p(x) = 0\text{for all} p\} .
$$

These two groups are connected together as follows:

$$
0\to \hat{E}_D(\mathbb{Q}) / \phi (E_{D'}(\mathbb{Q}))\to \mathcal{S}_\phi (E_{D'})\to \mathrm{III}(E_{D'})[\phi ]\to 0. \tag{9}
$$

Remark 2.2. By considering the dual isogeny  $\hat{\phi}$  instead, we get exact sequences analogous to  $(\overline{7})$ ,  $(\underline{8})$  and  $(\underline{9})$  which in turn give us the analogous definitions for  $S_{\hat{\phi}}(\hat{E}_D)$ ,  $\mathrm{III}(\hat{E}_D)$  and  $\mathrm{III}(E_D)[\hat{\phi} ]$

Remark 2.3. We also obtain exact sequences analogous to  $(\overline{7})$ ,  $(\underline{8})$  and  $(\underline{9})$  for  $\times 3 = \phi \circ \hat{\phi}$  which in turn give us the analogous definitions for  $S_{3}(E_{D'})$ ,  $\mathrm{III}(E_{D'})$  and  $\mathrm{III}(E_{D'})[3]$ , and similarly for  $\hat{E}_D$ .
