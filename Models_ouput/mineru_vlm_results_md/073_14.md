for all  $i^{\prime},i^{\prime \prime}\in \{1,\ldots ,|S|\}$  . Then, the equilibrium state  $\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})$  changes into  $\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}) + \mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y},\mathrm{d}x^{a|s})$  Here, note that  $\mathrm{d}M$  and  $\mathrm{d}p^{\mathrm{st}}$  are a matrix and a vector of order  $O(\mathrm{dx}^{a|s})$  , respectively. From the stationary state condition, both  $(E - M)p^{\mathrm{st}} = 0$  and  $(E - (M + \mathrm{d}M))(\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} + \mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}) = 0$  hold.

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {(\pmb {E} - (\pmb {M} + \mathrm{d}\pmb {M}))(\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} + \mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}) - (\pmb {E} - \pmb {M})\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = 0}\\ & {\Leftrightarrow (\pmb {E} - \pmb {M})\mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = \mathrm{d}\pmb {M}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} + \mathbf{1}\times O((\mathrm{d}x^{a|s})^2).} \end{array} \tag{A10}
$$

Here, the term of  $O((\mathrm{d}x^{a|s})^2)$  is small enough to be ignored. Then, the rest term  $\delta M p^{\mathrm{st}}$  is calculated as

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {(\mathrm{d}M\pmb {p}^{\mathrm{st}})_{i^{\prime}} = \sum_{i^{\prime \prime}}\mathrm{d}M_{i^{\prime}i^{\prime \prime}}p_{i^{\prime \prime}}^{\mathrm{st}} = \mathrm{d}M_{i^{\prime}i}p_{i}^{\mathrm{st}}}\\ & {\qquad = \mathrm{d}x^{a|s_{i}}p_{i}^{\mathrm{st}}y^{b|s_{i}}\times \left\{ \begin{array}{ll}1 - x^{a|s_{i}} & (s_{i^{\prime}} = ab s_{i}^{-})\\ -x^{a^{\prime}|s_{i}} & (s_{i^{\prime}} = a^{\prime}b s_{i}^{-},a^{\prime}\neq a)\\ 0 & (\mathrm{otherwise}) \end{array} \right..}\\ & {\qquad = \mathrm{d}x^{a|s_{i}}p_{i}^{\mathrm{st}}\left(p_{i^{\prime}}^{a|s_{i}} - \sum_{a^{\prime}}x^{a^{\prime}|s_{i}}p_{i^{\prime}}^{a^{\prime}|s_{i}}\right).} \end{array} \tag{A14}
$$

Thus,

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {(\pmb {E} - \pmb {M})\mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = \mathrm{d}x^{a|s_i}s_i^{\mathrm{st}}\left(\pmb{p}^{a|s_i} - \sum_{a'}x^{a'|s_i}\pmb{p}^{a'|s_i}\right)}\\ & {\mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = \mathrm{d}x^{a|s_i}s_i^{\mathrm{st}}(\pmb {E} - \pmb {M})^{-1}\left(\pmb{p}^{a|s_i} - \sum_{a'}x^{a'|s_i}\pmb{p}^{a'|s_i}\right)}\\ & {\Leftrightarrow \frac{\mathrm{d}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}}{\mathrm{d}x^{a|s_i}} = \frac{\partial}{\partial x^{a|s_i}}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathrm{Norm}(\mathbf{x}),\mathbf{y}) = p_{s_i}^{\mathrm{st}}(\pmb {E} - \pmb {M})^{-1}\left(\pmb{p}^{a|s_i} - \sum_{a'}x^{a'|s_i}\pmb{p}^{a'|s_i}\right)}\\ & {\Leftrightarrow \frac{\partial}{\partial x^{a|s_i}}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathrm{Norm}(\mathbf{x}),\mathbf{y})\cdot \pmb {u} = p_i^{\mathrm{st}}(\pmb {E} - \pmb {M})^{-1}\left(\pmb{p}^{a|s_i} - \sum_{a'}x^{a'|s_i}\pmb{p}^{a'|s_i}\right)\cdot \pmb {u}}\\ & {\Leftrightarrow \frac{\partial}{\partial x^{a|s_i}} u^{\mathrm{st}}(\mathrm{Norm}(\mathbf{x}),\mathbf{y}) = p_i^{\mathrm{st}}\left(\pi (\pmb{p}^{a|s_i},\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}) - \sum_{a'}x^{a'|s_i}\pi (\pmb{p}^{a'|s_i},\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y})\right).} \end{array} \tag{A16}
$$

The left- hand (resp. right- hand) side of Eq. (A19) corresponds to continualized MMGA (resp. MMRD).

# A.4 Proof of Theorem 4

Let us prove that X's strategy in Nash equilibrium is uniquely  $\mathbf{x} = x^{*}\mathbf{1}$  . First, we define  $u^{*}$  and  $v^{*}$  .

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & u^{*} = x^{*}y^{*}u_{1} + x^{*}(1 - y^{*})u_{2} + (1 - x^{*})y^{*}u_{3} + (1 - x^{*})(1 - y^{*})u_{4}\\ & \quad = \frac{u_{1}u_{4} - u_{2}u_{3}}{u_{1} - u_{2} - u_{3} + u_{4}}\\ & \quad (= -v^{*}) \end{array} \tag{A22}
$$

as X's and Y's payoffs in the Nash equilibrium in the zero- memory game. If X uses the Nash equilibrium strategy  $\mathbf{x} = x^{*}\mathbf{1}$  , the stationary state condition  $\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = M\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}$  satisfies

$$
\begin{array}{rl} & {\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = \left( \begin{array}{llll}x^{*}y_{1} & x^{*}y_{2} & x^{*}y_{3} & x^{*}y_{4}\\ x^{*}\tilde{y}_{1} & x^{*}\tilde{y}_{2} & x^{*}\tilde{y}_{3} & x^{*}\tilde{y}_{4}\\ \tilde{x}^{*}y_{1} & \tilde{x}^{*}y_{2} & \tilde{x}^{*}y_{3} & \tilde{x}^{*}y_{4}\\ \tilde{x}^{*}\tilde{y}_{1} & \tilde{x}^{*}\tilde{y}_{2} & \tilde{x}^{*}\tilde{y}_{3} & \tilde{x}^{*}\tilde{y}_{4} \end{array} \right)\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}}\\ & {\Rightarrow \pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}} = (x^{*}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\cdot \pmb {y},x^{*}(1 - \pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\cdot \pmb {y}),\tilde{x}^{*}\pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\cdot \pmb {y},\tilde{x}^{*}(1 - \pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\cdot \pmb {y}))^{\mathrm{T}}}\\ & {\Rightarrow \pmb{p}^{\mathrm{st}}\cdot \pmb {u} = u^{*}}\\ & {\Leftrightarrow u^{\mathrm{st}} = u^{*}.} \end{array} \tag{A26}
$$
