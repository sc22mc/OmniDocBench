For brevity, let us assume  $\mathcal{P}_0(z)\coloneqq \mathcal{P}_{0,0}(z)$  and  $\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}(z)\coloneqq 1 + \mathcal{P}_0(z)$ , then the horizontal parabolic region  $\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}(z)$  (see Fig. 2), is given by

$$
\Omega_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}}\coloneqq \{\omega \in \mathbb{C}:(\operatorname {Im}\omega)^2 < 3 - 2\operatorname {Re}\omega \mathrm{~or~}|1 - \omega |< 2 - \operatorname {Re}\omega \} .
$$

Since the major part of the above region lies in the left half plane, it is interesting to find the optimal radius of the domain disc for which it is fully mapped into the right half plane. Here below we define a class of analytic functions consisting of non- univalent functions.

Definition 1.1. Let the class  $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}}$  consist of functions  $f\in \mathcal{A}$  satisfying the subordination

$$
\frac{zf'(z)}{f(z)}\prec \mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}(z) = 1 - \frac{2}{\pi^2}\left(\log \left(\frac{1 + \sqrt{z}}{1 - \sqrt{z}}\right)\right)^2.
$$

Note that if  $f_0\in \mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}}$ , then it can be expressed as

$$
f_{0}(z) = z\left(\exp \int_{0}^{z}\frac{\mathcal{P}_{0}(t)}{t} dt\right), \tag{1.2}
$$

which acts as an extremal function for many radius results. Evidently, the subclasses of starlike functions  $S_{SG}^{*}$ ,  $S_{\mathcal{R}\mathcal{L}}^{*}$  and  $S\mathcal{L}^{*}(\alpha)$ $(0\leq \alpha < 1)$  (see Table  $\mathbb{I}$ ) are contained in  $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}}$ , however  $\mathcal{F}_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}}\not\in\mathcal{S}$ . In section 2, we examine some geometrical properties of the function  $\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}(z)$ .
