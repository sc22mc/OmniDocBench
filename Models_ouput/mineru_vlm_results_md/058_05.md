Lemma 2.5. Suppose  $a< 3 / 2$  and assume that  $\zeta (\eta)$  is defined as follows:

$$
\zeta = \zeta (\eta) = \log \left(\frac{\sqrt{\eta}}{\sqrt{1 - \eta}}\right)with\eta = \frac{e^{-\pi\sqrt{1 - 2a}}}{1 + e^{-\pi\sqrt{1 - 2a}}},
$$

then  $\mathcal{LP}(\mathbb{D})$  satisfies the following inclusion

$$
\mathcal{D}(a,r_a)\coloneqq \{\omega \in \mathbb{C}:\omega -a|< r_a\} \subset \Omega_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}},
$$

where

$$
r_{a} = \left\{ \begin{array}{cc}\sqrt{\left(a - \frac{3}{2} + \frac{2\zeta^{2}}{\pi^{2}}\right)^{2} + \frac{4\zeta^{2}}{\pi^{2}}}, & a\leq \frac{1}{2}\\ \displaystyle \frac{3}{2} -a, & \frac{1}{2} < a< \frac{3}{2}, \end{array} \right.
$$

Proof. We obtain a maximal disc centered at  $(a,0)$  , where  $a< 3 / 2$  , that can be inscribed within  $\Omega_{\mathcal{L}\mathcal{P}}$  . The distance from center  $(a,0)$  to the boundary  $f(\partial (\mathbb{D}))$  is given by square root of

$$
\mathcal{D}_a(X)\coloneqq \left(a + \frac{2}{\pi^2}\left(\log \left(\frac{\sqrt{X^2}}{\sqrt{1 - X^2}}\right)\right)^2 -\frac{3}{2}\right)^2 +\frac{4}{\pi^2}\left(\log \left(\frac{\sqrt{X^2}}{\sqrt{1 - X^2}}\right)\right)^2,
$$

where  $X = \cos t$  . Now the critical points of  $\mathcal{D}_a(X)$  are

$$
X^{\prime}\coloneqq \left\{ \begin{array}{ll}\pm \frac{e^{\frac{1}{2}\pi\sqrt{1 - 2a}}}{\sqrt{1 + e^{\pi\sqrt{1 - 2a}}}},\pm \frac{e^{-\frac{1}{2}\pi\sqrt{1 - 2a}}}{\sqrt{1 + e^{-\pi\sqrt{1 - 2a}}}}, & \mathrm{if~}a< 1 / 2,\\ \pm 1 / \sqrt{2}, & \mathrm{if~}1 / 2\leq a< 3 / 2. \end{array} \right.
$$

It can be verified that  $\mathcal{D}_a''(X) > 0$  at  $X = X^{\prime}$  , whenever  $a< 3 / 2$  . Therefore,  $X = X^{\prime}$  is the point of minima for  $\mathcal{D}_a(X)$  , which leads us to the optimal disk centered at  $a$  with radius  $r_a$
