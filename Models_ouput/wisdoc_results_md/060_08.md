Since j is an affine morphism, there are no higher direct images and we obtain followings :

$$
\mathrm{ch}E_{\cal L}=\pi_{2\,*}(\mathrm{ch}(j_{*}{\cal O}_{Z_{2}})\;\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{ch}{\cal L}\;\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{t}d{T}_{X})
$$

and

$$
\mathrm{ch}j_{*}{\cal O}_{Z_{2}}=j_{*}((\mathrm{td}{\cal N})^{-1})
$$

by applying the Grothendieck-Riemann-Roch theorem. Note that π2 ◦ j = ρ.

By the projection formula, we have:

$$
\begin{array}{rl}&{\mathrm{ch}E_{\mathcal{L}}=\pi_{2\,*}(\mathrm{ch}(j_{*}\mathcal{O}_{Z_{2}})\;\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{ch}\mathcal{L}\;\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{td}T_{X})}\\ &{\qquad=\pi_{2\,*}(j_{*}\mathrm{ch}(\mathrm{td}\mathcal{N})^{-1}\;\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{ch}\mathcal{L}\;\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{td}T_{X})}\\ &{\qquad=(\pi_{2\,*}\circ j_{*})(\mathrm{ch}(\mathrm{td}\mathcal{N})^{-1}\;j^{*}\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{ch}\mathcal{L}\;j^{*}\pi_{1}^{*}\mathrm{td}T_{X})}\\ &{\qquad=\rho_{*}((\mathrm{td}\mathcal{N})^{-1}\;q^{*}\mathrm{ch}\mathcal{L}\;q^{*}\mathrm{td}T_{X}).}\end{array}
$$

Consider the sheaf sequence

$$
0\rightarrow{\cal N}^{*}\rightarrow q^{*}\Omega_{X}\rightarrow{\cal O}_{E}(-E)\rightarrow0
$$

as given in [6, Lemma 2.1]. (Note that the morphism q in proposition 3.1 and in [6] are different morphisms.) Taking dual of this sequence, we obtain :

$$
0\rightarrow q^{*}T_{X}\rightarrow{\cal N}\rightarrow{\cal E}xt^{1}({\cal O}_{E}(-E),{\cal O}_{Z_{2}})\rightarrow0.
$$

Therefore we have

$$
(\mathrm{td}{\cal N})^{-1}=(q^{\ast}\mathrm{td}~T_{X})^{-1}\cdot(\mathrm{td}{\cal E}xt^{1}({\cal O}_{E}(-E),{\cal O}_{Z_{2}}))^{-1}.
$$

To evaluate tdExt1(OE(−E),OZ2), we consider the exact sequence :

$$
0\rightarrow{\cal O}_{Z_{2}}(-2E)\rightarrow{\cal O}_{Z_{2}}(-E)\rightarrow{\cal O}_{E}(-E)\rightarrow0.
$$

Taking the dual of this sequence, we have :

$$
0\to{\mathcal{O}}_{Z_{2}}(E)\to{\mathcal{O}}_{Z_{2}}(2E)\to{\mathcal{E}}xt^{1}({\mathcal{O}}_{E}(-E),{\mathcal{O}}_{Z_{2}})\to0.
$$

Therefore, we obtain

$$
\mathrm{td}{\cal E}xt^{1}({\cal O}_{E}(-E),{\cal O}_{Z_{2}})=\frac{2E}{1-e^{-2E}}/\frac{E}{1-e^{-E}}=\frac{2}{1+e^{-E}}
$$

and

$$
\begin{array}{rl}&{\mathrm{ch}E_{\mathcal{L}}=\rho_{*}\left(q^{*}\mathrm{ch}\mathcal{L}\cdot\frac{1+e^{-E}}{2}\right)}\\ &{\qquad=\rho_{*}\left(\bigg(1+\eta^{*}h_{1}+\frac{1}{2!}\eta^{*}h_{1}^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\eta^{*}h_{1}^{3}+\cdots\bigg)\cdot\bigg(1-\frac{1}{2}E+\frac{1}{4}E^{2}-\frac{1}{12}E^{3}+\cdots\bigg)\right)}\\ &{\qquad=\rho_{*}\left(1+\bigg(\eta^{*}h_{1}-\frac{1}{2}E\bigg)+\bigg(\frac{1}{2!}\eta^{*}h_{1}^{2}-\frac{1}{2}\eta^{*}h_{1}\cdot E+\frac{1}{4}E^{2}\bigg)+\cdots\right)}\\ &{\qquad=2+(H-\delta)+\bigg(\frac{1}{2}\rho_{*}\eta^{*}h_{1}^{2}-\frac{1}{2}\rho_{*}(\eta^{*}h_{1}\cdot E)+\frac{1}{4}\rho_{*}E^{2}\bigg)+\cdots.}\end{array}
$$

So we obtain c1(EL) = H − δ.
